Macros

2.2g of Protein per kg of lean body mass.

Use the Protein Digestibility Corrected Amino Score (PDCAA) for checking foods.

Choose Protein from Meat, Dairy and Eggs. Second is Legumes, Nuts, Peas, Broccoli and Spinach.

Meat increases testosterone levels.

Choose lean cuts of meat as fattier meats are harder to fit into meal plans.

Casein powder or Egg powder are good to use throughout the day. Where as Whey powder is only good post/pre workout.

Avoid Soy protein as it trigger Oestrogen.

Eat 30-40g of Protein per meal.

Eat complex sugar carbs over simple sugar carbs wherever possible.

You can rate food by their Glycemic index (lower is better).

Keep Salt as low as possible. Ideally less than 2,300g.

4700g of Potassium is required a day. You can obtain this through Broccoli, Fish, Meat, Sweet Potatoes etc.

Whole grains reduce risk of throat and mouth cancer.

Consume 14g of Fibre for every 1000kcal of food eaten.

Eat whole fruits instead of drinking juices.

Choose whole grain Bread, Rice, Cereals and Pasta.

Eat raw vegetables as snacks.

Squats

Position the Bar so its across the upper half of the Chest.

Face the Bar so you walk backwards and re-rack the Bar forwards.

Feet 20 degrees outward, shoulders tightened, lower back straight.

Put the Bar on the Bone at the top of your Shoulder Blades.

Stare at a single spot on the floor, shift the hips back and sit the Butt straight down while keeping the Chest up and entire back straight.

Knees no further than your Toes. Hips slightly lower than your Kneecaps and Back must be straight. The Bar should be aligned over the middle of the foot.

On the ascend push the hips upward and keep the Chest up.

Don't squat to rapidly. If the back rounds during squats, stop and stretch your Hamstrings.

Bench Press

Lie down on bench and retract Shoulder Blades. Arch the lower Back until a fist can fit in gap.

Your grip should be shoulder width apart, around 1-3 inches.

The Bar must be in the palm of your hand, grip it hard. Place Feet beneath the Knees.

Arms about 45 degrees relative to Torso, lower the Bar to the Chest (i.e: Nipples) and drive the Bar upwards.

Keep Elbows close to Torso the entire time, Butt must be kept on Bench, control the motion of the Bar.

Deadlift

Bar must be on the Floor.

Your stance must be narrower than your Shoulders and Toes must be pointed slightly out.

Stand up tall with your Chest out and take a deep breath into your Diaphragm, Brace the Abs.

Bend the Knees till your Shins touch the Bar and Knees slightly past it. Then lift your Chest until your Back is in a neutral position and is tight.

Arms must be straight and locked just outside of legs.

Grip the Bar by placing it into the middle of your palms.

Drive your Body upwards and slightly back as quick as possible via pushing the Heels. Ensure Hips and Shoulders move up.

Keep your Back neutral and tight the whole way up. The Bar should move up Shins and Roll over Knees and Thighs.

At the top keep the Chest out and the Shoulders back. Do not lean back or shrug the Weights or roll Shoulders.

Push Hips back and let Bar descend in a straight line until it reaches your Knees.

Bend the Knees and lower it to the shins while keeping back locked.
