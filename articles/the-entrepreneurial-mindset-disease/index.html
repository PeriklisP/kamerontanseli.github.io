The Entrepreneurial Mindset Disease

Having recently snapped out of the Entrepreneurial Mindset Disease. I have taken the liberty of writing an extensive overview of what I think is a huge problem within the Entrepreneur community.

Stage One: The plague of Instagram

Instagram caters itself to whatever will drive you onto the app more. This is part of what makes instagram an addicting and successful social media app.

Unfortunately business/entrepreneurial content is quite thrilling and addicting. I mean who wouldn’t want to be told they can be a millionaire by 35!

Because of this a lot of younger people, especially those who are struggling with money, get hooked into watching this sort of content. Instagram being Instagram continuously feeds this habit.

Stage Two: The paranoia of FOMO

Seeing others get filled with hope and start businesses is a great sight to behold. But it comes with a cost. If the person is in stage one of the disease they will begin to suffer from FOMO (Fear of missing out).

This will cause time anxiety and force the individual to give up their spare time in the devotion of one day building a successful business.

But in doing so they will also cause FOMO in others and the virus continues its cycle.

Stage Three: The paralysis of courses

Having forgotten about their hobbies or interests the individual will then fail to come up creative ideas. This will force the individual to look for ideas/help in pursuing an idea to build a business.

The causes of the disease, those how make the content in Stage One, will offer courses/books/podcasts sometimes for a price which will list out business ideas or markets to enter into.

Unfortunately the individual has no interest in said markets and won’t have the patience to sit through and provide a product of value to that niche community. Instead due to FOMO they will begin to get impatient as there’s no quick returns and buy more content to pursue other markets.

This will keep the individual in a constant cycle of paralysis in which they will make 0 progress in any chosen direction.

How to avoid falling into this trap.

Almost every great business started off with a founder that had a hobby. Whether it was telling their friends what they’re doing, collecting coupons or fiddling around trying to make computers.

It was an interest that enabled them to see the pain points of that specific niche community and build a great products that they themselves would love and work hard towards.

To avoid The Entrepreneurial Mindset Disease you must:

  1. Delete Instagram/Twitter or Unfollow entrepreneurial accounts.

  2. Tell your entrepreneurial friends that you don’t want to talk business when you meet up.

  3. Stop reading the news (it causes a load of anxiety).

  4. Take up a hobby in your new found spare time (Avoid feeling that you need to find a new idea within your hobby, instead just enjoy your hobby).

  5. Take regular breaks (1 week at time) where you don’t have your laptop etc.

  6. Enjoy your life and take it easy. Happiness is derived from gratitude not from desires.

A personal note of mine

I still continue to work in the evenings on tools. One of my most recent obsessions is bringing minimalism to the front end especially with our tooling/frameworks.

But I don’t let it consume my life. At anytime I could walk away from it and not feel anxiety that I’m wasting my time.

This is the key to contentfulness, I do have goals but I don’t dictate every minute of my time as to whether I’m hitting them or not.
